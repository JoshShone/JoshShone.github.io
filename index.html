Game Level Designers

What is a Game Level Designer?

Level designers understand what makes good gameplay. They design the game – but only a portion of it, normally referred to as a ‘level’. They take the specification defined by the gameplay designer, and get into the detail - the actions, events, objects and environment. They also design the characters and the ways they behave.

A good level designer will have a variety of skills that can place themselves on a multitude of different levels.
For example, one studio might ask for Experience creating levels with UE4 (White/Greyboxing levels in BSP). Experience of Level Design process (2D concept to 3D level creation). Gameplay passes (Area Collisions/Event Triggers/Out of bounds volumes etc). Experience with enemy placement/spawning/navigation and behaviours. Excellent communication skills to collaborate with a creative and multidisciplinary team. Enjoys a creative challenge with the passion and motivation to make great games. Experience using Unreal 4 Blueprints is a benefit. Experience with Maya is a benefit. Perforce / Hansoft skills is a benefit.
